Ways to Pay About

Outdated Currency Catalog

In exchange for coins that were once legal tender but aren't anymore, you are welcome to anything from this list of trinkets:

Potion, Very Rare

A jar that seems empty, yet bears a discernible weight to it. A creature can spend their bonus action to inhale the contents of this jar, gaining the following effects:

  • You have an additional failure box on your death saving throws.
  • As a bonus action when a creature is making death saving throws within 5 feet of you, you can go prone and give them advantage on their next death saving throw.
  • If a creature dies within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to give them your last breath. If you do so, you lose the effects of this item and you always have one failed death saving throw. The creature then regains a number of hit points equal to half of their hit point maximum.

Wondrous Item, Very Rare

The star resembles a lustrous blue moonstone less than 1 foot in diameter, and those who look upon it feel a strange sense of nostalgia. During a short rest, one creature can engage in a process similar to attunement in which they align with the star. Up to 6 people may be aligned with the star at any given time. Whenever a creature aligned to the star fails an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw within 120 feet of the star, the star gains one charge and gains an additional charge on a critical failure. While the star has 20 or more charges, a creature aligned with the stone can use it to add 10 to their roll. The stone then loses all of its charges.

Weapon (Mace), Very Rare

(requires attunement)

The mace is built from a sturdy, lightweight mithral grip and is topped with a spherical, cloudy moonstone. You gain a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this mace, and it additionally has the finesse property and is considered silver for the purpose of overcoming resistances and immunities.

Lunar Charges. Whenever you cast a spell of 1st level or higher while attuned to the mace, it gains a number of lunar charges equal to the spell slot level. You gain a bonus to your AC and saving throws equal to half of your lunar charges, rounded down. While holding the mace, you can spend any number of charges to reroll that many damage dice when you deal radiant damage with this mace or with a leveled spell.

The mace can store no more than 7 charges, and it loses all stored charges at the end of a long rest.

Crescent Strike. When you hit with a melee attack using it, you can expend up to 3 lunar charges. For each charge you expend, the target takes an extra 1d6 radiant damage.

Wondrous Item, Rare

You can designate a willing equine creature within 5 feet of you to be permitted entry into your horse pocket, doing so on their turn. The pocket is a private demiplane of a comfortable size for the creature, and the creature can stick their head out of the pocket at any point. Any equipment that the creature was carrying will go with them, but no objects other than their equipment can go inside of the pocket, and any other creatures that may have been smuggled in via bag of holding or similar items are teleported outside of the horse pocket to the nearest safe location. A creature can use their bonus action to get the equine creature out of the pocket.

Sewn onto an item of your choice!

Wondrous Item, Uncommon

This ordinary bag made from black cloth appears empty. Reaching inside the bag, however, reveals the presence of a small, bony object. The bag weighs 1/2 pound.

You can use an action to pull the bony object from the bag and throw it up to 20 feet. When the object lands, it transforms into a creature you determine by rolling a d8 and consulting the table. The creature vanishes at the next dawn or when it is reduced to 0 hit points.

The creature is friendly to you and your companions, and it acts on your turn. You can use a bonus action to command how the creature moves and what action it takes on its next turn, or to give it general orders, such as to attack your enemies. In the absence of such orders, the creature acts in a fashion appropriate to its nature.

Once three bony objects have been pulled from the bag, the bag can't be used again until the next dawn.

When you pull from the bag, you can make a DC 10+[the d8 number] Intelligence (Arcana) check to pull a specific result out of the bag. On a failure, the creature you summon becomes hostile towards you.

Wondrous Item, Rare

A small jar containing a fine power of ash collected from an Ocrian blue mangrove tree in a ritual process. This highly sought-after material has a number of uses:

  • It can be made into a soap, which when wetted and applied to an person can provide the effects of the lesser restoration spell. When used, roll a use die (d20). On a roll of 3 or below, the use die becomes a (d12). Each consecutive die size will shrink on a roll of 3 or below, and once a (d4) shrinks, the item is destroyed.
  • The ash can be added to ink and applied to a person in the form of a tattoo by a spellcaster with access to 6th level spell slots or higher. When done, the creature is attuned to the tattoo and cannot detune from it. While attuned to the tattoo, the creature has a +2 bonus to an ability score of their choice, which can exceed 20, and whenever they receive magical healing, they heal for an additional amount equal to half of the healing they would receive. (1.5 times the healing recieved)

Wondrous Item, Very Rare

This shadow is sentient and can understand any language that you speak. The shadow has the stats of a shadow from the Basic Rules, although it is not able to attack nor can it speak. It can move up to 15 feet away from you, either gliding 2-dimensionally along surfaces or rising up as a 3-dimensional, featureless, corporeal form. The shadow can be asked to aid in certain tasks similar to what an unseen servant can do by making a Charisma (Persuasion) check, with a DC varying based on complexity. It can additionally be taught skill and tool proficiencies over the course of 100 days instead of the standard 250.

Lumenophobia. The shadow has a fear of light, and it cannot willingly move within 10 feet of a light source. In addition, it cannot become a corporeal form in areas of bright light.

Staff, Rare

(requires attunement)

This wooden staff has veins of shimmering lapis rippling along its shaft and is topped with a small, fluffy cumulus cloud.

Spells. The staff has 10 charges. While holding it, you can spend the requisite number of charges to cast one of the following spells from the staff with your spell save DC: fog cloud (1 charge), warding wind (2 charges), wall of water (4 charges), watery sphere (6 charges), or wind walk (10 charges).

Cloud Platform. As a bonus action, you can create a solid cloud that is 10 feet x 10 feet. Creatures can move upwards through the platform as if it were air, and it can hold up to 4 creatures aloft. The cloud disperses at the end of your next turn.

The staff regains 1d4+4 spent charges at the end of a short rest, or 2d6 charges at the end of a long rest. If you expend the staff's last charge, roll a d20. On a 1, the staff dissipates into cloud vapour and is destroyed.

Wondrous Item, Very Rare

(requires attunement)

The item is a red velvet top hat that has been burned black on one side, right down the middle. While attuned to the hat, you gain the following benefits:

  • You gain the Evasion feature as per a rogue or monk
  • You gain a +2 bonus to AC
  • You gain a +2 bonus to all Saving Throws

Wondrous Item, Rare

You have a bushy fox tail with a white tip and a base coat color of your choice. While attached to you, your speed increases by 10 feet and you gain a +2 bonus to your AC.

Weapon (Greatsword), Artifact

(requires attunement)

This enchanted blade is taller than most humaniforms at 6.5 feet long; a brutally practical weapon only decorated by a crossguard depicting two wolf heads at either end. You gain a +2 bonus to attack and damage rolls made with this magic weapon. It is sized for a Large creature, dealing 4d6 slashing damage on a hit instead of 2d6.

Reckless Lunge. When you make an attack roll on your turn with this weapon, you can reduce your AC by 2 until the end of your turn. If you do, you are propelled up to 10 feet in any direction towards a creature that you can see before the attack roll is made. This reduction lasts until the start of your next turn.

Sentience. The Rending Hound is a sentient Lawful Good weapon with a 13 intelligence, 15 wisdom, and a 5 charisma. It has a +7 modifier on Wisdom (Perception) checks and makes such rolls at advantage. The sword understands common and can convey emotions to its wielder, but it's only outward perception of the world around it is through smell (range of 180 feet). While collecting information with its sense of smell, it produces an audible sniffing noise like that of a dog. The sword expresses enjoyment to being wielded, especially while being used to accomplish something, whether that be as a sword or for tracking a scent.

Wondrous Item(?), Common

This large, heavy luggage chest weighs around 500 pounds, including the chains padlocked around it (keys included in the purchase).
