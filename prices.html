How do I pay for things?

Here at the Ornate Treasury, we do not deal in common currency. Legal tender holds little appeal in the courts of the immortal fey, and they require a more particular intagibility. We call these Trinkets and Truths.

A trinket is an object that can be distinguished from a pile of them. If you cannot differentiate this item from a pile of other similar items, then it is not a trinket. Odd items, sentimental pieces, and many magic items qualify as trinkets.

A truth is an immaterial property about yourself. The more it is categorized, the less power it holds, and as such we have provided a list of truths that we have observed traded frequently:

  1. Your firstborn child (very famous example; no longer accepted in this establishment)
  2. Your first victory
  3. Your silver tongue
  4. Your last breath
  5. A relationship
  6. The sight from an eye
  7. One day of your life
  8. A day with your face
  9. Your welcome
  10. Your name
  11. Your home
  12. A favour
  13. Your dreams
  14. Your latest lie
  15. Your sense of balance
  16. Your luck
